Crescent Cove

The final year project created during my time at Confetti, this game went on to shortlist for Nottingham's Young Creative Awards.

What was the project?
This project was the final year CIR, which stands for 'Creative Industry Response', we were given an assigned creative task - which was to create a promotional game for the national history museum towards the target audience of ages 16-25. We went down the route of archeology abstracted from dinosaurs, which was a theme present on the museum's' website. The full-indepth analysis of the brief and the different various themes presented and why dinosaurs were specifically chosen is over on my legacy college portfolio site.

What this project taught me
Teamwork: This Project taught me vital skills about working in a team, as this was my first collaborative project - where I worked with just one other Games Artist, I stuck to handling the project itself and roughly prototyping the mechanics ahead of time to get playtesting and feedback in. Once the feedback was received and people were happy I would go back and polish up these mechanics to make them more optimised, streamlined and user-friendly

Planning: Crescent Cove also heavily emphasised the importance of a structurally sound plan from the beginning, as this project was the first project I had planned out this much and this proved beneficial as we had a clear vision from the beginning which made our next steps easier to follow, and this opened my eyes to the importance of planning.
